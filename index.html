📚 First Things First — What Is an Annuity?
📖 Plain English Explanation
An annuity is a contract between you and an insurance company. You give them a lump sum of money, and they promise to either grow it safely, pay you income every month, or both — depending on which type you choose.
🏦 Think of it like this: You put money into a special account with an insurance company. Depending on which type you pick, it either grows safely every year — OR it pays you a steady monthly check for the rest of your life, even if you live to 100. You can never outlive the money. That is the big idea.
🌱 Why Do People Choose Annuities?
1. They are worried about running out of money in retirement. An annuity can guarantee income for life.

2. They want their savings to grow safely without the stock market putting their money at risk.

3. They want to leave money to their family when they pass away, with guarantees.
🎯 Simple version: An annuity is like a personal pension you build yourself. You put money in, it grows or pays out, and you stay financially safe no matter what happens in the economy.

📖 Words You May Hear — Decoded
Premium
The money you put in upfront.
💬 Just means your deposit or investment amount.
Surrender Charge
A fee if you withdraw too much too early (usually first 5–10 years).
💬 Like a penalty for breaking a contract early — similar to a bank CD penalty.
Tax-Deferred
Your money grows without being taxed each year.
💬 The government waits to collect taxes until you take money out — your savings grow faster.
Fixed Indexed Annuity (FIA)
Earns interest based on a market index but you can never lose your principal.
💬 Some of the market upside with a guarantee you never lose your deposit.
MYGA
Multi-Year Guaranteed Annuity — a fixed rate for a set number of years.
💬 Like a CD at a bank but usually with a better rate and no annual taxes.
Rider
An optional add-on benefit like a lifetime income guarantee.
💬 Think of it like an upgrade feature you add to your annuity.
Beneficiary
The person who receives your annuity money if you pass away.
💬 Who gets the money — usually your spouse or children.
Free Withdrawal
Most annuities let you take up to 10% per year without any penalty.
💬 You are not completely locked in — you can access some money each year fee-free.
SPIA
Single Premium Immediate Annuity — you put in a lump sum and income starts right away.
💬 Deposit money in, monthly checks start coming out — immediately.
AM Best Rating
A financial strength grade for insurance companies. A++ is the highest possible.
💬 Like a credit score for the insurance company — the higher, the safer your money is.
